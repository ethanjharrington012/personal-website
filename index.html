Expirence

Not you orderinery background

From when I can remember I was working with my Father. He ownes an aborist company in Maine called Blue Spruce. before that When I was 12. I remeber going to the fairs and seeing these old dudes with big beards pounding on red hot metal. I for sure thought they were wizards! In some way they were. They took something unuseful and ugly. and turned it into something useful and beautiful. I loved it. So when I was 12 my Father bought me an anvil. I then did everything I could to try to find a mentor who was willing to teach me this craft. luckily I did. I fell in love with blacksmithing. All I did was read about it, watch documentarys about it, and practice it. I then started a little company out of it called Frog Forge. I had buisness cards and even an etsy side. I sold my things at craft fairs and did custom orders. After that I started working for my father full time. Which would be after high school. We worked 10-11 HR days. Brutal in some ways but romantic in others. But I know I don't want to do that line of work forever. I love to learn, and read. Once I get fixated one something and find if somewhat iteresting theres no stoping from there.
